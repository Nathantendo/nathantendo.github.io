Mission Log: Submarine Survival

STATUS: CRITICAL // DEPTH: UNKNOWN

1. The Submarine: Your "Home" and "Hazard"

Since the player is stuck inside the sub, the vessel should feel like a character itself.

  • The Stress Loop: You want to go deeper, but depth increases pressure. Pressure causes leaks. Leaks require repairs.
  • Station Management: Instead of just "driving," the player might have to physically move between the Sonar (to see), the Engine (to move), and the Hull (to weld shut holes).

2. The Monsters: Audio over Visuals

In the deep ocean, you usually hear something before you see it.

  • Bio-luminescence: Maybe the monsters are pitch black except for glowing lures or eyes.
  • The Sound Trap: Perhaps some monsters are blind but track the sound of your engine or the clanging of your repair hammer.

3. The "Depths" Progression

How does the game change as you go deeper?

[attachment_0](attachment)
  • Photic Zone (Surface): Bright, colorful, but smaller, faster enemies.
  • Midnight Zone: Total darkness. You rely entirely on floodlights and sonar.
  • The Deep Trenches: This is where the "boss" monsters live.

4. The Repair Mechanic

To make repairs feel urgent, they shouldn't just be a loading bar.

  • Resource Management: Do you use your last piece of metal to fix a leak, or to reinforce the engine so you can run away faster?
  • Flooding: If a room floods, you lose access to the equipment in that room until you pump the water out.
